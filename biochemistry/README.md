## Quick Reference - Most Testable Patterns

**For rapid review before practice blocks:**

### Charge Rules
- pH < pI → positive charge | pH > pI → negative charge
- Glu↔Lys = ±2 (largest charge change)

### Enzyme Kinetics
- Competitive: Km↑ Vmax same (overcome with ↑[S])
- Noncompetitive: Km same Vmax↓ (cannot overcome)

### Compartmentalization
- Cytosol: Glycolysis, FA synthesis, PPP
- Mitochondria: β-oxidation, TCA, ETC, urea cycle (partial)
- Liver/kidney only: Gluconeogenesis (need G6Pase)

### Clinical Patterns
- Sepsis + lactate + O₂ = mitochondrial dysfunction
- Fasting hypoglycemia + hepatomegaly = Von Gierke (G6Pase)
- Refeeding = hypophosphatemia (ATP depletion)
- ↑MMA = B12 deficiency (specific)

## 0. Essential Amino Acid Functional Groups

### Charge Classification (pH 7)

**Basic (+)**: Lys, Arg, His
- His pKa ~6 → proton shuttle at pH 7

**Acidic (−)**: Asp, Glu

**Polar uncharged**: Ser, Thr, Tyr (phosphorylation); Cys (disulfides); Asn, Gln

**Hydrophobic**: Leu, Ile, Val, Phe, Met, Trp, Ala

### Charge-Change Rule
- **Largest change**: Glu ↔ Lys = ±2
- **Conservative**: Hydrophobic ↔ hydrophobic (minimal disruption)

---

## 1. Protein Structure

### Secondary Structure
- **α-helices and β-sheets**: Stabilized by **backbone H-bonds**
- **Rule**: Side chains do NOT stabilize secondary structure → **eliminate**
- **Pro disrupts α-helices**: No H on nitrogen
- **Gly provides flexibility**: Small side chain

### Tertiary Structure
- Stabilized by: hydrophobic interactions, ionic bonds, disulfide bonds, H-bonds between side chains

### Quaternary Structure
- Multiple polypeptide subunits
- **Rule**: Monomeric proteins lack quaternary structure

### Collagen (High Yield)
- Every 3rd residue = Gly (tight triple helix)
- **Vitamin C hydroxylates Pro/Lys** → stable helix
- **Cu²⁺ for cross-linking** (lysyl oxidase)

**Diseases**:
- **Scurvy**: Vit C deficiency → bleeding gums, poor wound healing
- **Ehlers-Danlos**: Hyperextensible skin, joint hypermobility
- **Osteogenesis imperfecta**: Brittle bones, blue sclera

---

## 2. Protein Folding & Degradation

### Heat Shock Proteins
- **ATP-dependent chaperones**
- Refold misfolded proteins during stress

### Misfolding
- Exposes **hydrophobic residues**
- Leads to aggregation: Alzheimer's (Aβ), Parkinson's (α-synuclein)

### Proteasome
- **Ubiquitin tags** → 26S proteasome degrades
- **Rule**: Ubiquitin → proteasome (cytosolic)

### Disulfide Bonds
- **Oxidizing environment** (ER, extracellular)
- Stabilize: insulin, antibodies, structural proteins
- **Rule**: Oxidizing agents stabilize; don't "stimulate" folding

---

## 3. Post-Translational Modifications (Essential Only)

### Phosphorylation
- Adds negative charge to Ser, Thr, Tyr
- Can activate OR inhibit:
  - **Glycogen phosphorylase**: Phosphorylation → ACTIVE
  - **Glycogen synthase**: Phosphorylation → INACTIVE

---

## 4. Enzyme Kinetics

### Michaelis-Menten
- **Km** = [S] at ½ Vmax (affinity)
  - Low Km = high affinity
- **Vmax** = kcat × [Enzyme]

### Inhibition Patterns
| Type | Km | Vmax | Lineweaver-Burk |
|------|-----|------|-----------------|
| **Competitive** | ↑ | Same | Intersect y-axis |
| **Noncompetitive** | Same | ↓ | Intersect x-axis |
| **Uncompetitive** | ↓ | ↓ | Parallel lines |

**Clinical examples**:
- Competitive: Ethanol for methanol poisoning
- Irreversible: Aspirin (COX), organophosphates (AChE)

### Allosteric Regulation
- **Sigmoidal curve** = cooperativity
- **Feedforward**: F-1,6-BP activates pyruvate kinase
- **Feedback**: ATP/citrate inhibit PFK-1

**Rule**: Saturated enzyme = enzyme-limited (need more enzyme, not substrate)

## 5. Metabolism as ICU Physiology

### Fed vs Fasted States

**Fed (Insulin)**:
- ↑ Glycolysis, glycogen synthesis, FA synthesis
- ↓ Gluconeogenesis, glycogenolysis, β-oxidation
- **F-2,6-BP** activates PFK-1, inhibits F-1,6-BPase

**Fasted (Glucagon)**:
- ↑ Gluconeogenesis, glycogenolysis, β-oxidation
- ↓ Glycolysis, glycogen synthesis, FA synthesis
- ↓ F-2,6-BP (opposite)

**Prolonged Fasting (>48h)**:
- Primary fuel: **fatty acids**
- Brain adapts to **ketone bodies**

---

## 6. Glycolysis & Gluconeogenesis

### Three Irreversible Steps & Bypasses

| Glycolysis | Gluconeogenesis Bypass |
|------------|------------------------|
| Hexokinase/Glucokinase | **G6Pase** |
| **PFK-1** | **F-1,6-BPase** |
| Pyruvate kinase | **PEPCK + Pyruvate carboxylase** |

### Rate-Limiting Enzymes
- **Glycolysis**: PFK-1
  - Activated: AMP, F-2,6-BP
  - Inhibited: ATP, citrate
- **Gluconeogenesis**: F-1,6-BPase
  - Inhibited: F-2,6-BP, AMP

### Tissue Rules
- **Muscle**: Glycolysis only (lacks G6Pase, PEPCK)
- **Liver/Kidney**: Full gluconeogenic machinery

---

## 7. Glycogen Metabolism

### Breakdown
1. **Glycogen phosphorylase** → G1P
2. **Debranching enzyme** → α-1,6 branches
3. **Phosphoglucomutase** → G1P ⇄ G6P

**Rule**: Release = phosphorylated monomers (G1P), not free glucose

### Muscle vs Liver
- **Muscle**: No G6Pase → local energy only
- **Liver**: Has G6Pase → releases glucose to blood

### Hormonal Regulation
- **Insulin**: Activates synthase (dephosphorylation)
- **Glucagon/Epi**: Activates phosphorylase (phosphorylation via cAMP→PKA)

### Storage Diseases (High Yield)
| Type | Enzyme | Features |
|------|--------|----------|
| **I (Von Gierke)** | G6Pase | Fasting hypoglycemia, hepatomegaly |
| **V (McArdle)** | Muscle phosphorylase | Exercise intolerance, myoglobinuria |

---

## 8. Fat Metabolism

### Lipolysis (Adipose)
- Triglyceride → 3 FA + glycerol
- Activated: Glucagon, epinephrine
- **Rule**: Glycerol release = lipolysis (NOT oxidation)

### Carnitine Shuttle (Critical)
- **CPT I**: Acyl-CoA + carnitine → acyl-carnitine
- Inhibited by **malonyl-CoA** (fed state)
- **MCAT trap**: FAs need carnitine to enter mitochondria

### β-Oxidation (Mitochondria)
- FA → acetyl-CoA + NADH + FADH₂
- **Rule**: "Fat oxidation" = acetyl-CoA, NADH, FADH₂

### Malonyl-CoA Regulation
- **High** (fed): Promotes FA synthesis, blocks β-oxidation
- **Low** (fasted): No FA synthesis, β-oxidation proceeds

---

## 9. Ketogenesis

### When It Occurs
- **β-oxidation exceeds TCA capacity** (fasting, DKA)

### Ketone Bodies
1. Acetoacetate
2. β-Hydroxybutyrate (predominant)
3. Acetone (exhaled, fruity breath)

### Tissue Use
- **Brain**: Uses ketones during fasting
- **Liver**: CANNOT use its own ketones (lacks enzyme)

**MCAT loves**: "Why doesn't liver use ketones?" → Missing succinyl-CoA transferase

---

## 10. TCA Cycle & ETC

### TCA Products (per acetyl-CoA)
- 3 NADH, 1 FADH₂, 1 GTP, 2 CO₂
- **Rate-limiting**: Isocitrate dehydrogenase

### ETC Complexes
- **I**: NADH → CoQ
- **II**: FADH₂ → CoQ (no H⁺ pump)
- **III**: CoQ → Cyt c
- **IV**: Cyt c → O₂
- **V (ATP synthase)**: H⁺ gradient → ATP

### Inhibitors (High Yield)
| Inhibitor | Target | Effect |
|-----------|--------|--------|
| **Rotenone** | Complex I | Parkinsonism |
| **Cyanide, CO** | Complex IV | Lactic acidosis, death |
| **Oligomycin** | ATP synthase | Blocks ATP synthesis |

### Uncouplers
- **DNP**: Dissipates gradient → heat, no ATP
- **UCP1**: Brown fat thermogenesis

---

## 11. Pentose Phosphate Pathway

### Purpose
1. **NADPH** (biosynthesis, antioxidant)
2. **Ribose-5-P** (nucleotides)

### G6PD Deficiency
- ↓ NADPH → cannot reduce glutathione → oxidative stress
- **Hemolytic anemia** (sulfonamides, antimalarials, fava beans)
- Heinz bodies, bite cells

---

## 12. Redox Balance

### Glutathione System
- **GSH + H₂O₂ → GSSG + H₂O** (glutathione peroxidase)
- **GSSG → GSH** (glutathione reductase, uses NADPH)

**Rule**: RBCs rely on PPP for NADPH (no mitochondria)

---

## 13. Amino Acid Metabolism

### Transamination
- Amino acids → **glutamate** (via aminotransferases, need B6)
- Glutamate → α-KG + NH₃ (glutamate dehydrogenase)

### Glucogenic vs Ketogenic
- **Glucogenic**: Most AAs → glucose
- **Ketogenic only**: **Leu, Lys** (cannot form glucose)
- **Both**: Ile, Phe, Trp, Tyr, Thr

**MCAT trap**: Eliminate Leu/Lys for "which AA → glucose"

### One-Carbon Metabolism
- Homocysteine + methyl-THF → methionine (needs **B12**)

**Diseases**:
- **Homocystinuria**: Tall, lens dislocation, Marfan-like
- **B12 deficiency**: ↑ homocysteine, ↑ MMA
- **Folate deficiency**: ↑ homocysteine, normal MMA

---

## 14. Urea Cycle

### Compartments
- **Mitochondria**: CPS I, OTC
- **Cytosol**: ASS, ASL, arginase

### Clinical
- **Hyperammonemia**: Neurotoxic (crosses BBB)
- **OTC deficiency**: ↑ ammonia, ↑ orotic acid
- **BUN**: Protein catabolism + renal clearance

---

## 15. Nucleotide Metabolism (Essential)

### Purine Salvage
- **HGPRT**: Hypoxanthine/guanine + PRPP → IMP/GMP

### Lesch-Nyhan Syndrome
- HGPRT deficiency → ↑ uric acid, self-mutilation, gout

### Chemotherapy Targets
| Drug | Mechanism |
|------|-----------|
| **Methotrexate** | Inhibits dihydrofolate reductase → megaloblastic anemia |
| **5-FU** | Inhibits thymidylate synthase |
| **6-MP** | Purine analog |

---

## 16. Heme Metabolism (Essential)

### Synthesis
- **ALA synthase** (rate-limiting, needs **B6**)
- Ferrochelatase inserts Fe²⁺ → heme

### Lead Poisoning
- Inhibits ALA dehydratase + ferrochelatase
- Microcytic anemia, basophilic stippling

### Jaundice
**Unconjugated**: Hemolysis, Gilbert, Crigler-Najjar, neonatal
**Conjugated**: Biliary obstruction, Dubin-Johnson

**MCAT trap**: Dark urine = conjugated (can be excreted)

---

## 17. Vitamins & Cofactors

### Water-Soluble (High Yield)
| Vitamin | Cofactor | Key Enzyme | Deficiency |
|---------|----------|------------|------------|
| **B1** | TPP | PDH, α-KG-DH | Beriberi, Wernicke-Korsakoff |
| **B2** | FAD | Succinate DH | Cheilosis |
| **B3** | NAD⁺ | Dehydrogenases | Pellagra (3 Ds) |
| **B6** | PLP | Aminotransferases | Neuropathy, sideroblastic anemia |
| **B7 (Biotin)** | Biotin | Carboxylases | Rare |
| **B9 (Folate)** | THF | Thymidylate synthase | Megaloblastic, neural tube defects |
| **B12** | Cobalamin | Methionine synthase, MMA mutase | Megaloblastic, ↑ MMA |
| **C** | Ascorbate | Prolyl/lysyl hydroxylases | Scurvy |

### Fat-Soluble
| Vitamin | Function | Deficiency |
|---------|----------|------------|
| **A** | Vision (rhodopsin) | Night blindness |
| **D** | ↑ Ca²⁺ absorption | Rickets, osteomalacia |
| **E** | Antioxidant | Hemolytic anemia (rare) |
| **K** | Clotting factor γ-carboxylation | Bleeding |

### Clinical
- **Wernicke-Korsakoff**: Thiamine deficiency (alcoholism)
- **Isoniazid**: Depletes B6 → neuropathy
- **Warfarin**: Inhibits vitamin K epoxide reductase

## 18. Membrane Structure (Essential)

### Cholesterol Effects
- **High temp**: Restricts movement → ↓ fluidity
- **Low temp**: Prevents packing → ↑ fluidity
- **Rule**: Cholesterol = fluidity buffer

### Fatty Acid Saturation
- **Saturated**: Rigid → ↓ fluidity
- **Unsaturated**: Kinks → ↑ fluidity

### Lysosomal Storage Disease (High Yield)
**Tay-Sachs**: Hexosaminidase A deficiency, GM2 accumulation, cherry-red macula, death by 3-4 years

---

## 19. Signal Transduction

### GPCRs
- **Gs**: → adenylyl cyclase → ↑ cAMP → PKA
  - Hormones: Epinephrine (β), glucagon
- **Gq**: → phospholipase C → IP3 + DAG
  - IP3 → Ca²⁺ release; DAG → PKC

### Receptor Tyrosine Kinases
- **Ras/MAPK**: Proliferation, differentiation
- **PI3K/AKT**: Glucose uptake, protein synthesis, survival

**Insulin signaling**:
- Insulin → RTK → IRS → PI3K → AKT
- **AKT**: GLUT4 translocation, glycogen synthesis, ↓ gluconeogenesis

### Steroid Hormones
- Diffuse through membrane → intracellular receptor → nucleus → transcription

---

## 20. Membrane Transport

### Active Transport
**Primary** (uses ATP):
- **Na⁺/K⁺-ATPase**: 3 Na⁺ out, 2 K⁺ in
  - Inhibited by **digoxin**

**Secondary** (uses gradient):
- **Na⁺/glucose (SGLT)**: Symporter

### Digoxin Mechanism
- Inhibits Na⁺/K⁺-ATPase → ↑ Na⁺ → ↓ Na⁺/Ca²⁺ exchange → ↑ Ca²⁺ → stronger contraction

---

## 21. Acid-Base Balance (Essential)

### Henderson-Hasselbalch
```
pH = pKa + log([A⁻]/[HA])
```

### Metabolic Acidosis
**High anion gap (MUDPILES)**:
- **M**ethanol, **U**remia, **D**KA, **L**actic acidosis, **E**thylene glycol, **S**alicylates

### Respiratory Compensation
- Metabolic acidosis → hyperventilation (Kussmaul) → ↓ PCO₂

---

## 22. Clinical Integration

### Hypoglycemia Differential
**Fed**: Insulin overdose, insulinoma
**Fasted**: G6Pase deficiency, cortisol deficiency, liver failure, alcohol

### Lactic Acidosis
**Type A** (hypoxia): Shock, CO poisoning
**Type B** (metabolic): Metformin, thiamine deficiency, PRIS, liver failure

### Refeeding Syndrome
- Insulin surge → **hypophosphatemia** → ATP depletion → cardiac/respiratory failure

---

## 23. High-Energy Molecules

### ATP Hydrolysis
- ΔG = −7.3 kcal/mol
- Stabilized by: resonance, charge relief, entropy

### Other Compounds
| Compound | ΔG | Stabilization |
|----------|-----|--------------|
| **PEP** | −14.8 | Resonance + tautomerization |
| **Creatine-P** | −10.3 | Resonance only (NO tautomerization) |

**Rule**: "Tautomerization" for creatine → **eliminate**

### Mg²⁺ Requirement
- Stabilizes ATP phosphates
- Required for all ATP-dependent reactions

---

## 24. Lab Techniques (Essential)

### Blotting
- **Western**: Proteins (antibody)
- **Northern**: RNA (probe)
- **Southern**: DNA (probe)

### SDS-PAGE
- Separates by **size only**
- SDS denatures + uniform negative charge

### Chromatography
- **Gel filtration**: Large elute first
- **Ion exchange**: Separates by charge

### PCR
- Requires **Taq polymerase** (heat-stable)

---

## 25. Peptide Bonds

### Stability
- **Thermodynamically favorable** to hydrolyze
- **Kinetically stable** (high Ea)
- **Requires proteases**

**Rule**: Denaturants disrupt noncovalent bonds; peptide bonds intact unless proteolysis

## Elimination Playbook (Apply Fast)

### Protein & Structure
1. Side chains do NOT stabilize secondary structure → **eliminate**
2. Monomeric protein → no quaternary structure → **eliminate**
3. Oxidizing agents stabilize (form disulfides); "stimulate folding" → **eliminate**
4. Peptide bonds intact after denaturation → kinetic barrier

### Metabolism
5. Lipolysis vs oxidation: Glycerol release ≠ oxidation products
6. Muscle lacks G6Pase → cannot release glucose
7. Liver cannot use its own ketones → lacks enzyme

### Enzyme Kinetics
8. Competitive: Km↑ Vmax same; overcome with ↑[S]
9. Noncompetitive: Km same Vmax↓; cannot overcome
10. Saturated enzyme → enzyme-limited

### Thermodynamics
11. ΔG cues: Same enzyme both ways → ΔG ≈ 0
12. Catalysts lower Ea, NOT ΔG

### Compartmentalization
13. Glycolysis = cytosol; β-oxidation = mitochondria
14. Gluconeogenesis = liver/kidney only
15. Urea cycle = mitochondria + cytosol

### Vitamins
16. Thiamine = PDH; deficiency → lactic acidosis
17. B12 deficiency → ↑ MMA (specific)
18. NADH = catabolism; NADPH = anabolism/antioxidant

### Clinical
19. Sepsis + lactate + O₂ = mitochondrial dysfunction
20. Refeeding = hypophosphatemia
21. Fasting hypoglycemia + hepatomegaly = G6Pase deficiency
22. CN/CO = Complex IV

## Summary

This framework covers **80-90% of MCAT biochemistry** through **pattern recognition**:

1. **Identify mechanism** (kinetics, pH, compartment)
2. **Apply elimination rule**
3. **Eliminate ≥2 wrong answers**
4. **Select clinically consistent mechanism**

**Goal**: 128-132 through mechanistic reasoning, not memorization.
